Exploration

Some people go for nice hikes. Given the choice, I'd rather be on a rainy bushwhack through slide alder, or sneaking through a bebb willow thicket somewhere looking for cool bugs. I'm not sure whether this is the visible tip of some iceberg of subconscious masochism, but I justify it in all kinds of fun ways.

I like to imagine that no one else has ever been where I'm standing. It takes hard work to imagine that well, given the walmart bags and antique tin cans we've scattered through the forest here and everywhere. I'm committed, though, or should be, because I'll walk for hours just to get a little farther from the road than I did last time.

Ecology

My fascination with the natural world is born mostly of my inability to pay attention to things that aren't moving, when other things are. Birds, bugs, rodents, large mammals, snails that appear suddenly after the first spring rain. I don't know all their names, but I know some of them well enough to think of them as neighbors.

Eating Stuff

And what could make a weird slimy thing more interesting, than if you could turn it into a delicious meal? And then eat it? Quick omelettes full of pine spikes or lactarius, crawfish etouffee from invasive mudbugs, tassel-eared tamales... It would be hard to choose a favorite.

Interestingly, in the process of putting as many oddball organisms as I could find on my kitchen table, I learned a good deal about how to find, identify, and cook them all.
